Where it all begins

How we started

From a home kitchen
to Lagos' favourite

EDIP Kitchen started as a passion project — a small home kitchen in Lagos where one family's love for authentic Nigerian food became something much bigger. What began as baking cakes for friends and neighbours quickly grew into a full-fledged homemade food business.

Every item on our menu — from our fluffy cakes to our golden chin-chin — is still made by hand, with the same care and recipes we started with. No shortcuts, no preservatives, just real homemade food.

A word from us

The people behind
every bite

EDIP Kitchen is a family name and a family mission. We grew up eating the foods we now make — zobo on hot afternoons, chin-chin during the holidays, meat pies fresh from the oven. We wanted to share that feeling with Lagos.

Running a homemade food business is hard work. But every message from a happy customer, every reorder, every "this tastes just like home" — that's what keeps us going. We're grateful for every single one of you.

"We don't just sell food. We sell the feeling of home on a plate."

— The EDIP Kitchen Family
